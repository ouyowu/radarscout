# RadarScout Product Hunt Launch Page

Use this file as the copy source for the Product Hunt launch form.

Product URL:

https://radarscout.io

Primary conversion goal:

Get indie founders, agencies, and SaaS marketers to try the free plan and add their first 3 monitoring keywords.

Positioning:

RadarScout finds high-intent Reddit leads by monitoring buying signals and scoring each mention with AI.

Do not position this as a spam, auto-reply, or auto-DM tool. Product Hunt users are sensitive to anything that sounds like automation spam. The story should be: "I built a cleaner way to find useful Reddit conversations without manually checking Reddit every morning."

## Maker Comment

Post this as the first maker comment after launch.

```text
Hey Product Hunt,

I built RadarScout because I was tired of manually checking Reddit every morning for customer conversations.

Most keyword alert tools send everything: memes, random mentions, complaints, and threads that have nothing to do with buying intent. That gets noisy fast.

RadarScout does one simple thing differently:

It monitors Reddit for your keywords, then uses AI to score each mention from 1-10 based on purchase intent.

So instead of seeing every mention, you can focus on posts where someone is:

- asking for a tool recommendation
- looking for an alternative to a competitor
- comparing solutions
- describing a painful workflow
- actively trying to buy or hire

The free plan includes 3 keywords forever, with no credit card required.

I am especially curious about feedback from founders, agencies, and indie hackers:

What keywords would you monitor for your own product?

And what would make a Reddit alert useful enough that you would actually reply to it?
```

## Comment Reply Templates

Use these to answer launch comments naturally.

### If someone asks how it is different from F5Bot

```text
F5Bot is great for simple keyword alerts. The main difference is filtering.

RadarScout tries to answer: "Is this mention actually worth replying to?"

So every post gets an intent score based on whether the person is asking for recommendations, comparing alternatives, describing a painful problem, or showing signs of buying/hiring intent.
```

### If someone asks whether this is spam automation

```text
No, and I am intentionally avoiding that direction.

RadarScout does not auto-post, auto-DM, or auto-reply. It only helps you find relevant public conversations and understand why they might be worth replying to.

The reply still has to be human and useful.
```

### If someone asks what sources are next

```text
Reddit is the first source because it has a lot of public buying-intent conversations.

The next architecture step is Campaigns + Sources, so one campaign can eventually track Reddit, public job/task requests, communities, and other demand signals.

I want to keep it compliant and human-in-the-loop, especially for marketplaces.
```

### If someone asks who it is for

```text
The best fit right now is:

- indie SaaS founders
- agencies looking for client leads
- consultants selling a specific service
- marketers tracking competitor alternatives

It works best when you know the exact phrases your buyers use when they are in pain or comparing tools.
```

### If someone asks about free plan limits

```text
The free plan includes 3 keywords forever and does not require a credit card.

That is enough to test one competitor alternative keyword, one category keyword, and one pain-point keyword.
```

## Pre-Launch Checklist

Do these before submitting:

- Confirm `https://radarscout.io` loads.
- Confirm sign-up works.
- Confirm a new user can add at least one keyword.
- Confirm crawler logs show non-zero keywords after a keyword is added.
- Confirm pricing page works.
- Confirm Product Hunt gallery images are ready.
- Confirm logo is square and readable.
- Prepare a list of 20-30 people who can give feedback on launch day.
- Prepare social posts.
- Prepare maker comment.

## Launch-Day Timeline

Recommended time zone strategy:

Product Hunt resets based on Pacific Time. Launch early in the Product Hunt day if possible.

Suggested schedule:

- T-1 day: prepare page, images, video, social copy.
- Launch hour: submit product, immediately add maker comment.
- First 2 hours: respond to every comment.
- First 6 hours: share to warm contacts and founder communities asking for feedback.
- Rest of day: answer comments, collect objections, improve homepage copy if repeated confusion appears.

## What Not To Do

Do not:

- Ask people to upvote directly.
- Use fake accounts.
- Mass-post the same pitch across Reddit.
- Promise automated Reddit replies.
- Describe RadarScout as a bot that replies for users.
- Lead with "free, no credit card" only.
- Hide the actual product behind vague AI language.

The clean story:

> RadarScout helps founders find Reddit conversations that are actually worth replying to.
