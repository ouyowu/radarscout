## The Apartment Smart Lock Problem

Landlords say no to replacing locks. Leases prohibit modifications. Move-out inspections demand original hardware. Yet you still want keyless entry, remote access, and the ability to let friends in without hiding keys under doormats.

Traditional smart locks replace your entire deadbolt. That's a non-starter for renters. But several smart locks install without removing existing hardware, leaving your apartment completely reversible when you move.

This guide covers five options that work for apartments, from $100 retrofit devices to $250 premium solutions. All install in under 30 minutes, require no drilling, and restore to original condition in minutes.

## What Makes a Lock Apartment-Friendly?

**Non-destructive installation**: No drilling into door or frame  
**Reversible**: Remove it completely when you move  
**Keeps existing keys**: Landlord's keys still work  
**No exterior changes**: Outside looks completely normal  
**Portable**: Take it to your next apartment

The best apartment locks install on your deadbolt's interior side only. The exterior remains unchanged—your landlord's master key works exactly as before.

## Installation: What to Expect

### August/Wyze Process (15 minutes):

1. Remove interior deadbolt mounting screws
2. Place August/Wyze adapter plate
3. Attach lock body over adapter
4. Tighten mounting screws
5. Attach faceplate
6. Install batteries
7. Complete app setup

No tools beyond included screwdriver needed.

### Level Process (20 minutes):

1. Remove entire interior lock mechanism
2. Install Level mechanism in its place
3. Ensure proper alignment with deadbolt
4. Secure with provided screws
5. Install battery
6. Complete app setup

Requires Phillips screwdriver (included).

### Yale Process (20 minutes):

1. Similar to August process
2. Additional step: mount interior keypad
3. Connect keypad to lock body via cable
4. Complete app setup and code programming

Slightly more complex due to keypad.

## Lease-Friendly Considerations

### Check Your Lease for These Clauses:

**"No lock modifications"**: Retrofit locks don't modify locks—they attach to them. But confirm with landlord.

**"Must provide landlord with keys"**: Explain that landlord's existing keys work unchanged.

**"No permanent installations"**: These qualify as temporary—completely reversible.

**"No drilling/damage"**: These use existing screw holes only.

Most landlords approve retrofit locks when you explain they're reversible and preserve master key access. Get written approval to avoid disputes.

### Document Everything:

**Before installation**: Photograph your original lock from all angles.

**After removal**: Store original hardware safely. You'll need it when moving.

**At move-out**: Reinstall original hardware. Your door should look identical to move-in photos.

## What NOT to Buy

### Standard Smart Locks (Not Retrofit):

August, Yale, Schlage, Nest all make excellent smart locks—that replace your entire deadbolt. These require landlord permission and leave permanent modifications.

### DIY Smart Lock Kits:

Raspberry Pi and servo motor setups are fun projects but unreliable for daily security. Stick to commercial products for locks.

### Keyless Entry Keypads (Standalone):

Keypads that mount to your door with adhesive or screws are visible modifications. Use interior-mounted options instead.

## Common Questions

**Q: Will my landlord's master key still work?**  
A: Yes, for August/Wyze/Yale retrofit models. Your exterior lock remains completely unchanged.

**Q: What happens if batteries die?**  
A: Keys always work. Most locks warn you weeks before battery death. Keep spare batteries handy.

**Q: Can I take the lock when I move?**  
A: Absolutely. That's the entire point. Takes 5 minutes to uninstall and reinstall original hardware.

**Q: Will my door frame/hardware work?**  
A: August/Wyze include adapter plates for most common deadbolts. Check compatibility on manufacturer websites before buying.

**Q: Do these work on double-deadbolt doors?**  
A: Most work on standard single-cylinder deadbolts. Double-cylinder locks (key on both sides) are trickier—check specific model compatibility.

**Q: What about fire safety?**  
A: All these locks have manual override. You can always unlock from inside by turning the mechanism manually.

## Security Considerations

Smart locks don't make your door more or less physically secure. Your deadbolt's physical security remains unchanged. Smart locks add:

**More secure**: Digital access logs, no lost keys to worry about, instant access revocation  
**Less secure**: Potential for electronic failure, reliance on batteries, vulnerable to phone theft

Overall security is similar to traditional locks, with added convenience and access control benefits.

## Installation Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Not checking deadbolt compatibility**  
Measure your deadbolt before buying. Most work with standard residential deadbolts, but confirm.

**Mistake 2: Over-tightening screws**  
Especially with August/Wyze mounts. Over-tightening can strip threads or crack plastic adapters.

**Mistake 3: Ignoring door alignment**  
If your door doesn't close smoothly now, a smart lock won't fix that. Adjust door alignment first.

**Mistake 4: Relying solely on auto-unlock**  
Auto-unlock is convenient but not 100% reliable. Always test manual unlock before relying on automation.

**Mistake 5: Forgetting to save original hardware**  
You'll need it when you move. Store in a labeled bag somewhere safe.
