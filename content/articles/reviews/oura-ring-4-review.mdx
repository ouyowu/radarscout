## The Pitch

Oura Ring markets itself as the ultimate sleep and recovery tracker wrapped around your finger instead of your wrist. No screen, no distractions, just continuous health monitoring that disappears into your daily routine. For $349 plus $6/month, you get sleep stage tracking, readiness scores, heart rate variability analysis, and temperature monitoring—all from a device that looks like slightly chunkier wedding band.

The question isn't whether the Oura Ring works. It clearly does. The question is whether the insights it provides justify the cost and commitment.

After three weeks wearing Generation 4 24/7, here's what we found.

## What It Actually Is

The Oura Ring 4 is a health tracking device shaped like a ring. The hardware includes:

**Sensors packed inside**:
- 7 temperature sensors (new for Gen 4)
- Red and infrared LEDs for heart rate and SpO2
- Infrared photoplethysmography for blood oxygen
- 3D accelerometer for movement

**Physical specs**:
- Titanium shell in silver, black, stealth, gold, or rose gold
- Weighs 4-6 grams depending on size
- 2.55mm thick
- IP68 water resistant
- Sizes 6-13 (US sizing)

**Battery and charging**:
- 4-7 day battery life (typically 5-6 days in real use)
- Charges in 20-80 minutes
- Proprietary charging dock included

The ring connects to your smartphone via Bluetooth. All data lives in the Oura mobile app (iOS and Android). There's no display on the ring itself—just a small LED that pulses during charging.

## The Setup Experience

Oura requires you to order a sizing kit before buying the actual ring. This matters because ring sizes are permanent—no adjustable bands here. The kit arrives in 3-5 days, you wear the plastic sizers for 24 hours to account for finger swelling, then order your actual size.

When the real ring arrives, pairing takes maybe 90 seconds. Open the Oura app, follow prompts, place the ring in the charger, and it connects. You answer basic questions (age, weight, height, biological sex), and the ring starts collecting data immediately.

First night's sleep data appears the next morning. Daytime activity tracking starts right away. HRV and temperature baselines establish over 14-60 days depending on the metric.

No complaints here. Setup is straightforward, and the sizing kit prevents expensive sizing mistakes.

## Sleep Tracking: Where Oura Shines

This is what you buy the Oura Ring for. Sleep tracking is comprehensive, detailed, and actionable.

### What It Tracks

Every morning, the app presents:

**Sleep Score** (0-100): Aggregate score combining total sleep, efficiency, REM/deep sleep balance, latency, and timing. Scores above 85 = optimal. Below 70 = suboptimal.

**Sleep stages**: How much time in light, deep, REM, and awake periods throughout the night. Presented as both percentages and time duration.

**Sleep timing**: When you went to bed, when you woke up, and how this aligns with your circadian rhythm.

**Efficiency**: Percentage of time in bed actually spent sleeping. 85% or higher is considered good.

**Latency**: How long it took to fall asleep after getting into bed.

**Heart rate variations**: Your heart rate patterns during sleep, including lowest resting heart rate.

**HRV during sleep**: Heart rate variability measured while you sleep, a key recovery indicator.

**Temperature deviation**: How your body temperature deviated from your personal baseline. Useful for detecting illness early or tracking menstrual cycles.

**Respiratory rate**: Breaths per minute while sleeping.

### How Accurate Is It?

We compared Oura's sleep tracking against a Whoop band worn simultaneously for 10 nights. Agreement on total sleep time was within 5-15 minutes most nights. Sleep stage breakdowns varied more—Oura sometimes called deep sleep what Whoop called light sleep and vice versa.

This matches published research: consumer sleep trackers correctly identify sleep stages about 70-80% of the time compared to polysomnography (medical-grade sleep studies). Not perfect, but trends over weeks are reliable even if individual nights have some misclassification.

The temperature tracking is unique among sleep wearables. Our ring detected a fever 12 hours before symptoms appeared—body temperature spiked +0.8°C above baseline on a night when we felt fine, then woke up actually sick the next morning.

### What the Data Actually Tells You

Oura doesn't just dump data at you. The Sleep Score distills everything into one number you check in the morning. High score? You slept well. Low score? Here's why and what might help.

The app also identifies contributors and detractors. "Your sleep was improved by good sleep timing and efficiency, but limited by low REM sleep." This framing makes it clear what helped and what hurt.

Over time, patterns emerge. We noticed consistently low REM sleep on nights after drinking even one glass of wine. We noticed deep sleep increased on days with morning exercise. These insights are genuinely useful.

## Readiness Score: Should You Train Today?

The Readiness Score (0-100) tells you how recovered your body is and how much stress it can handle that day.

**Inputs that affect readiness**:
- Previous night's sleep quality
- Resting heart rate trends
- HRV balance
- Body temperature
- Previous day's activity
- Overall recovery balance

High readiness (85+) means your body is ready for intense training or stressful days. Low readiness (below 70) suggests taking it easy, focusing on recovery, or at minimum not pushing hard in the gym.

We tested this by ignoring the readiness score deliberately. Trained hard on a 62% readiness day? Felt terrible during the workout and needed two days to recover instead of one. Rested on an 88% readiness day and trained the next day? Felt strong and recovered normally.

The readiness score isn't mystical. It's just your body telling you whether you've recovered from recent stress. But having a number that consolidates all the signals helps prevent overtraining or illness.

## Activity Tracking: The Ring's Weakness

Let's be clear: the Oura Ring is not a fitness tracker.

It tracks:
- Steps and distance (reasonably accurate)
- Active calories burned
- Heart rate during activities (when you manually log a workout)
- Activity time periods

It doesn't track:
- GPS routes
- Real-time workout feedback
- Specific exercises or workout types automatically
- Detailed workout metrics

If you want workout tracking, buy a smartwatch or Whoop. The Oura Ring knows you exercised and approximately how intense it was, but provides no guidance during the workout and limited detail after.

This isn't a flaw—it's intentional design. Oura focuses on recovery, not performance. The ring tells you whether you recovered from yesterday's workout, not how to optimize today's training.

## The Subscription Question

Here's the controversial part: you need an Oura Membership ($5.99/month) to access most features.

**What you get without membership**:
- Three daily scores (Sleep, Readiness, Activity)
- Basic sleep time and movement data

**What requires membership**:
- Detailed sleep stage analysis
- HRV and temperature trends
- Workout heart rate
- Long-term trends and patterns
- All the insights that make Oura useful

Oura includes one free month of membership, then charges monthly. You can use the ring without subscribing, but you're paying $349 for very basic functionality.

Our take: If you're buying the ring, budget for the subscription. Without it, you'd be better off with a $100 fitness tracker.

Is $6/month reasonable? That's $72/year on top of the $349 hardware. Over three years, you're spending $565 total. Compare this to Whoop ($30/month) or premium gym memberships ($100+/month), and Oura's subscription feels proportional to the value provided.

But some people hate subscriptions on principle. If that's you, Oura won't change your mind.

## Comfort and Wearability

We wore the ring 24/7 for three weeks, including:
- Sleep (obviously)
- Showers
- Weightlifting
- Typing on keyboards
- Cooking
- Swimming

**Comfort**: After day two, you forget it's there. The titanium doesn't irritate skin. The smooth interior doesn't catch on anything. The ring survived everything without scratches.

**Finger swelling**: This is real. Hot days, high-sodium meals, and altitude changes all caused temporary finger swelling. On those days, the ring felt snug. Not painful, just noticeable. This resolved within hours as swelling decreased.

**Impact on activities**: Typing felt normal. Lifting weights felt normal (though we switched to a different finger during deadlifts to avoid pressure on the barbell). The ring never interfered with daily tasks.

**Sizing note**: Order true to size or slightly larger. A ring that's too tight becomes uncomfortable when you swell. A ring that's slightly loose stays comfortable and doesn't affect sensor accuracy noticeably.

## Battery Life in Real Use

Oura claims 4-7 days. We got 5-6 days consistently with all features enabled including continuous heart rate, automatic activity detection, and blood oxygen monitoring.

Charging from empty to full took 65-75 minutes. Most people charge while showering or working at a desk, so the ring never actually dies unless you forget multiple days in a row.

The charging dock is small and portable. It uses USB-C. No complaints here—battery life is long enough to not think about it most days.

## The Mobile App

The Oura app is clean, fast, and well-organized. Three tabs (Today, Readiness, Sleep) present your key scores prominently. Deeper dives into each metric are one tap away.

**What we liked**:
- Daily scores summarize everything instantly
- Trends view shows weekly and monthly patterns
- Moment tags let you correlate activities with metrics
- Explore section explains what each metric means

**What could improve**:
- No web dashboard (mobile app only)
- Limited third-party integrations beyond Apple Health and Google Fit
- No family sharing or multiple profiles
- Export features exist but could be more flexible

The app does everything necessary and does it well. It's not groundbreaking, but it's functional and pleasant to use daily.

## Who Should Buy the Oura Ring 4

**Strong fit for**:
- Sleep optimization enthusiasts
- People who find wrist wearables uncomfortable during sleep
- Anyone wanting discreet 24/7 health tracking
- Cycle tracking and fertility awareness users
- Recovery-focused athletes who don't need real-time workout feedback
- People wanting to detect illness early

**Poor fit for**:
- Active athletes needing detailed workout metrics
- Anyone on a tight budget (cheaper alternatives exist)
- Subscription-averse buyers
- People wanting smartwatch features (notifications, apps, etc.)
- Users with fingers that swell significantly and frequently

## Comparison to Alternatives

**vs. Whoop**: Whoop offers superior workout tracking and recovery-based training guidance. Oura offers better sleep tracking and temperature monitoring in a more comfortable form factor. Choose based on whether you prioritize training (Whoop) or sleep (Oura).

**vs. Apple Watch**: Apple Watch is a full smartwatch with comprehensive features. Oura is a single-purpose health tracker. If you want one device for everything, buy an Apple Watch. If you want best-in-class sleep tracking in a comfortable form factor, buy Oura.

**vs. Budget fitness trackers**: Fitbit and Garmin offer sleep tracking for $150-250 without subscriptions. The data is less detailed, but 70-80% as useful. If Oura's price seems excessive, start with a Fitbit Charge and upgrade only if you need more detail.

## The Verdict

The Oura Ring 4 is the best sleep tracking wearable you can buy if—and only if—you value sleep insights enough to spend $349 plus ongoing subscription fees for them.

The hardware is excellent. The sensors are sophisticated. The data is detailed and actionable. The form factor truly does disappear on your finger in a way wrist wearables never match.

But the value proposition depends entirely on what you do with the insights. If you're serious about sleep optimization, the Oura Ring provides information that leads to genuine improvements in sleep quality and recovery. If you just want to know you slept poorly after a bad night, a $100 fitness tracker tells you that.

**Our recommendation**: If you've tried cheaper sleep trackers and found them insufficient, Oura delivers the depth you're looking for. If you're new to sleep tracking, start cheaper and upgrade to Oura if you outgrow the basics.

The Oura Ring 4 earns its reputation as the sleep tracking king. But kings come with royal prices.
