Smart locks have graduated from novelty gadgets to practical home security upgrades — but the two most popular options take fundamentally different approaches. The **August Smart Lock Pro** converts your existing deadbolt into a connected device without replacing anything visible from outside. The **Schlage Encode Plus** is a full lock replacement that adds a touchscreen keypad and Apple Home Key support. Both cost under $300, both are well-made, and both will frustrate you if you pick the wrong one for your situation.

Here is a complete breakdown to help you decide.

## Design & Build

The August Smart Lock Pro's defining characteristic is that **nobody outside your door knows it exists**. The exterior of your door looks completely unchanged — the same deadbolt hardware, the same keyhole, the same lockset. Only the interior thumb-turn is replaced by August's cylindrical smart module, which is compact and relatively unobtrusive. For renters who cannot modify exterior hardware or homeowners who want a seamless exterior look, this is a meaningful advantage.

The trade-off is that the August module sits on the interior side of the door only, which means no keypad. Entry is through the August app, auto-unlock via your phone's location, voice commands through Alexa/Siri/Google, or Z-Wave integration with a smart home hub. You can add a separate August Keypad accessory for around $60, but that is an extra cost and extra installation.

The **Schlage Encode Plus** is a complete lock replacement. You remove your existing deadbolt entirely and install the Schlage in its place. The result is a finished, polished look on both sides of the door — a touchscreen keypad on the exterior and a clean thumb-turn on the interior. The built-in keypad means you never need your phone to get in, which is a real practical advantage for households with kids, frequent guests, or anyone who occasionally leaves the house without their phone.

Schlage's **ANSI Grade 1** certification is the highest available for residential locks, meaning it has been tested to withstand more forced entry attempts and environmental stress than the Grade 2 August. For security-focused buyers, that rating difference matters.

**Winner:** Schlage Encode Plus for build quality and all-in-one functionality. August Smart Lock Pro for renters and retrofit scenarios.

## Smart Home Integration

Both locks work with Apple HomeKit, Amazon Alexa, and Google Home — but the quality of that integration is not equal.

The **August Smart Lock Pro** requires the separate **August Connect Wi-Fi Bridge** (included in the Pro version) to get remote access and HomeKit support. Without the bridge, the lock only works over Bluetooth, meaning you need to be within range to operate it. With the bridge, you get full remote control, but you now have an additional device to power and maintain. HomeKit integration on August is functional but adds a step of complexity.

The **Schlage Encode Plus** has **Wi-Fi built directly into the lock**. There is no hub, no bridge, no secondary device. Plug it in (metaphorically — it runs on four AA batteries), connect it to your Wi-Fi during setup, and it is immediately accessible remotely. The native HomeKit support is clean, and it is one of the few locks that supports **Apple Home Key** — the feature that lets you unlock your door by holding your iPhone or Apple Watch near the lock, the same way you tap to board a flight. No app launch, no Face ID, just tap and enter.

For Google Home users, both locks work well. For Amazon Alexa users, both support voice commands to lock (though Alexa cannot unlock by default for security reasons). For Z-Wave smart home setups like SmartThings or Home Assistant, the August Pro has a clear edge since Z-Wave is built in.

**Winner:** Schlage Encode Plus for most smart home setups, especially Apple HomeKit and Home Key users. August Smart Lock Pro for Z-Wave hubs.

## Access Management

The **August Smart Lock Pro** manages all access through the August app. You can create virtual keys for guests (time-limited or permanent), see an activity log of who came and went, and receive notifications when the door locks or unlocks. It is a clean system, but it is entirely app-dependent — guests need the August app installed to use their virtual key.

The **Schlage Encode Plus** stores up to **100 access codes** directly in the lock's hardware. These codes work whether your Wi-Fi is up, whether your phone has battery, and whether the Schlage app is installed. Guests simply punch in a code on the keypad. You can still manage codes through the Schlage Home app and schedule temporary codes for housecleaners or Airbnb guests, but the lock functions fully without any of that.

For households that frequently have guests, trades workers, or anyone less comfortable with apps, the Schlage's hardware-based code system is dramatically more practical.

**Winner:** Schlage Encode Plus — the built-in keypad and hardware code storage offer more reliable everyday access.

## Battery Life

Both locks run on four AA batteries, but their lifespans differ.

- **August Smart Lock Pro:** approximately 3–6 months, depending on how frequently the lock operates and whether the Connect bridge is active.
- **Schlage Encode Plus:** approximately 12 months, owing to more efficient Wi-Fi circuitry and a larger battery compartment.

Both will send low-battery notifications through their apps well before power runs out. Neither should leave you locked out by surprise if you pay attention to notifications.

**Winner:** Schlage Encode Plus.

## Who Should Buy Which

**Buy the August Smart Lock Pro if:**
- You are renting and cannot replace the exterior lock hardware
- You already have a Z-Wave smart home hub
- You prefer to keep your existing deadbolt and physical key arrangement unchanged
- You want the minimal-profile interior-only approach

**Buy the Schlage Encode Plus if:**

## Verdict

The **Schlage Encode Plus** is the more capable, more secure, and more practical smart lock for homeowners — especially those in the Apple ecosystem. Built-in Wi-Fi, Apple Home Key, 100 hardware access codes, and ANSI Grade 1 certification make it an exceptional all-in-one upgrade for $299.

The **August Smart Lock Pro** earns its place for a specific and important scenario: renters, people who cannot modify exterior hardware, and Z-Wave users who want to retrofit smart access without disturbing their existing deadbolt setup. Its app-based virtual key system is clean and reliable, and the Connect bridge delivers solid remote access.

Know your situation before you buy.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I install either lock myself, or do I need a locksmith?**
Both locks are designed for DIY installation. The August Smart Lock Pro is particularly simple — most users complete installation in under 10 minutes since it only replaces the interior thumb-turn. The Schlage Encode Plus requires removing your existing deadbolt and installing a new one, which typically takes 20–30 minutes with a screwdriver. No locksmith is required for either.

**Q: Does the Schlage Encode Plus work without Wi-Fi?**
Yes. If your Wi-Fi goes down, the Schlage Encode Plus still works via Bluetooth (short range) and the built-in touchscreen keypad with stored access codes. You lose remote access and app notifications until Wi-Fi is restored, but the lock is fully functional locally.

**Q: Will the August Smart Lock Pro work with my existing deadbolt?**
The August Smart Lock Pro is compatible with most standard single-cylinder deadbolts. It attaches to the interior thumb-turn using one of several included adapters. Compatibility is very broad, but August maintains a lock compatibility checker on their website for unusual hardware. Multi-point locks and certain European-style locks are generally not compatible.

**Q: What is Apple Home Key and why does it matter?**
Apple Home Key is a feature in iOS 16 and later that stores your home access credential in Apple Wallet. When your iPhone or Apple Watch is near a compatible lock, you simply hold the device close — there is no need to open an app, authenticate with Face ID, or tap a button. It is the same NFC-based experience as tapping to pay at a register. The Schlage Encode Plus is one of the few locks that support it natively.

**Q: Is the August Smart Lock Pro compatible with Airbnb hosting?**
Yes, and it works well for this use case. You can create time-limited virtual keys through the August app and set them to expire automatically. This is a common and popular use case for August locks in rental properties where you want keyless, code-free guest access that expires on checkout.
